Spoken Digit Prediction

In this project, I developed a machine learning model capable of predicting digits spoken in audio files. The project utilizes the free-spoken-digit-dataset from Jakobovski's GitHub repository, specifically designed for ease of use as the audio equivalent of the MNIST dataset for handwritten digit recognition. The audio files in the dataset were short sound clips, each under one second in duration. Two model architectures were experimented with: a Feedforward Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) and a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). Both models use spectrogram images of audio files as input.

MQTT-Based Remote Alert System

In this project, I developed a remote notification system using two Raspberry Pi devices and the MQTT protocol. One Raspberry Pi acts as a sensor monitor, while the other controls a physical switch. Whenever the state of the switch changes, an MQTT message is sent to the second Pi, triggering a response. The notification Pi manages an LED board, with a red LED indicating an alert (switch pressed) and a blue LED showing normal operation (switch not pressed). This system provides an efficient wireless monitoring solution.

Zero Emission Gas Well

Spring 2021-Fall 2021

The project was sponsored by Nadal & Gussman, a natural gas company located in Ruston, LA. The company desired a solution to reduce natural gas emissions at well sites. During operation of the pneumatic devices, natural gas is emitted to the environment. The major constituent of natural gas is methane, which is a greenhouse gas hazardous than carbon dioxide. The engineering team successfully fabricated a solution which included a storage thank that supplied compressed air to operate the devices, instead of the natural gas previously used. This design completely eliminates the emissions from pneumatic device operation. A remote area power supply (RAPS) system was implemented into the design, as the locations of the gas well-sites do not have access to the power grid.
